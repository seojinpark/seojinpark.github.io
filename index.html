About me

I’m a postdoc associate at MIT Computer Science & Artificial Intelligence Lab, where I am advised by Prof. Mohammad Alizadeh.

My research interest is on improving latency, scalabitility and consistency of large-scale distributed systems, such as in-memory databases, blockchain, or DNN training systems.

I received a Ph.D. in Computer Science from Stanford University in 2019, where I was advised by Prof. John Ousterhout. My dissertation is on achieving both low latency and strong consistency in large-scale systems. For my PhD study, I was supported by Samsung Scholarship (2013-2018).

I received a B.S. in Computer Science and in Mathematics, and a M.Eng in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from MIT in 2013. For my undergraduate study, I was supported by STX Scholarship (2008-2012).
